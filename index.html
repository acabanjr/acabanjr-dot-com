As a kid, I had a book full of “My Inventions,” a binder divided into various categories. It was full of my ideas. Detailed mock-ups ranging from clothing designs to video game concepts, and even blueprints for skyscrapers. I’ve always been creative and even more so, organized. These adjectives define my character and guide my approach to even the smallest details.

FASHION DESIGN

My nearly two-decade long career in fashion began with the decision to embrace creative roles over technical ones. Despite initial concerns about their lucrative differences, that choice has continued to enrich my creative spirit, allowed me to channel my passion into various avenues, and has proven advantageous.

As a fashion designer, I conceptualize collections from inspiration to technical and illustrative sketches, for boys, men, and young men. I’m also skilled in graphic, print, fabric, and technical design. Throughout my career, I've contributed to a diverse assortment of projects. Designing and marketing a men’s fashion accessory brand, building and maintaining a custom-fashion e-commerce website and fashion blog, refining design processes, and designing business headquarters.

INTERIORS

My design eye extends beyond fashion and into interiors. My space, whether at home or at the office will almost always be the neatest, cleanest, and most thought-out. If my career hadn’t taken me down the path of fashion, it would have most certainly taken me towards interior design.

Interior design has been important to me for as far back as I can remember. As an adolescent living in his parent’s place, I'd regularly repaint, redecorate, and rearrange my room. The same was true living alone, and it's true now living with my wife.

In a professional capacity, I've been responsible for the design and supervision of multiple office space projects, as well as contributing to the creation of various furniture designs.

TECHNOLOGY

"What do you want to be when you grow up?" The response to this question varied when I was a child. My earliest memory of a desired career was a cartoonist. I used to draw a lot, and it's something I regret abandoning. I am a very creative person; however, I am not an artist. I might pick it up again someday. Another past career desire that comes to mind is an architect, but I eventually homed in on, "a computer programmer."

I don't necessarily believe things come naturally to people. I've always been tech-savvy, but that’s likely only true because I tinker, and dive into settings, organically learning along the way. It drives me a bit crazy how many of us sit in front of a computer every day and manage to still have so little understanding of even the basics. I guess essentially, it just comes down to what we care to care about.

I enjoyed the programming courses I took in high school and college, but ultimately decided against becoming a programmer. It’s a supposed lucrative career, but that wasn’t enough of an incentive for me to make it such a large part of my being. Technology in general however, continues to come “natural” to me and plays a huge role in efficiently executing so much of my creative desires. I designed and built this very website in just an evening using modern technologies available to everyone.
